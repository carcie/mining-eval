# User Manual: BTM Financial Model

## Overview

The **BTM Financial Model** application is a  financial simulation tool designed to evaluate the profitability of a "Behind-the-Meter" (BTM) cryptocurrency mining operation. Rather than assuming the future is a straight line, this tool uses historical market data and stochastic (randomized) simulations to stress-test your facility against the highly volatile realities of crypto markets.

---

## 1. Hardware Configuration (Sidebar)

This section defines the physical mining machines (ASICs) that will be deployed in your facility. The physical efficiency of these machines is the primary defense against market downturns.

* **ASIC Model:** The specific hardware unit you plan to deploy. The dropdown includes a library of normalized units across different algorithms.
* **Hashrate (TH/s):** * *Definition:* Terahashes per second. The computational speed of a single machine.
* *Context:* Higher hashrate means a larger share of the network's block rewards. We normalize all algorithms to TH/s to maintain mathematical consistency in the engine.


* **Power (W):** * *Definition:* The electrical draw of a single machine in Watts.
* *Context:* Used alongside Hashrate to calculate the machine's efficiency (Joules per Terahash, or J/TH). Lower J/TH means the machine does more work using less electricity.


* **Unit Cost ($):** * *Definition:* The purchase price of a single machine (CAPEX).
* *Context:* Drives the initial negative cash flow at Month 0. You can override library defaults to reflect bulk discounts or changing secondary market prices.



---

## 2. Facility Specs (Sidebar)

This section defines the physical and contractual constraints of the power plant hosting the miners.

* **Max Power Cap (MW):** * *Definition:* Megawatts. The absolute maximum amount of electricity the facility can draw from the plant at any given moment.
* *Context:* This dictates your **Fleet Size**. The application will automatically calculate exactly how many ASICs fit into this power envelope.


* **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness):** * *Definition:* A ratio of Total Facility Power to IT Equipment Power. A PUE of 1.0 means 100% of power goes to the miners. A PUE of 1.03 means 3% of your power budget is consumed by cooling, networking, and lighting.
* *Context:* A higher PUE reduces the amount of power available for actual mining, forcing you to buy fewer machines and reducing total revenue.


* **Energy Price ($/kWh):** * *Definition:* The fixed cost of electricity per kilowatt-hour.
* *Context:* This is your operational baseline. If the revenue generated by 1 kWh drops below this price, the facility is operating at a loss.


* **Fixed O&M ($/mo):** * *Definition:* Operations and Maintenance.
* *Context:* Fixed monthly overhead costs (security, internet, staff salaries, insurance) that do not scale with energy usage.



---

## 3. Market Calibration & Risk Parameters

This module dictates how the future is modeled. Because crypto is volatile, we use a Monte Carlo simulation—running thousands of parallel "futures" to see where the project most commonly ends up.

* **Initial Hashprice ($/PH/day):** * *Definition:* The expected daily revenue for 1 Petahash of computing power on Day 1 of the project.
* *Context:* This is the starting line for the simulation. All future revenue paths branch out from this number.


* **Horizon (Months):** * *Definition:* The lifespan of the project.
* *Context:* Mining hardware depreciates rapidly. This sets the boundary for how long the machines have to pay themselves off before their value goes to zero.


* **Annual Difficulty Growth (%):** * *Definition:* The rate at which the global mining network becomes more competitive.
* *Context:* As more miners plug in globally, your fixed slice of the pie gets smaller. This applies a continuous "downward gravity" (negative drift) to your expected revenue.


* **WACC (Discount Rate %):** * *Definition:* Weighted Average Cost of Capital.
* *Context:* A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow. This rate discounts future cash flows back to present value, accounting for inflation and the risk premium of the investment.


* **Calibrate Volatility via yfinance:** * *Definition:* A toggle that downloads the last 12 months of actual Bitcoin price data to calculate real-world volatility.
* *Context:* Instead of guessing how wild the market swings will be, this anchors the simulation's "randomness" to actual historical market behavior.


* **Monte Carlo Paths:** * *Definition:* The number of distinct future timelines the engine will simulate.
* *Context:* 5,000 paths means the engine calculates the project's lifespan 5,000 times, each with slightly different randomized market shocks, to build a statistical consensus.

## 4. Understanding the Dashboard & Results

Once the engine runs, it aggregates the thousands of futures into actionable metrics and visualizations.

### **Top-Line Metrics**

* **Median NPV (Net Present Value):** The "middle-of-the-pack" financial outcome. If this is positive, the project is expected to generate a return higher than your WACC.
* **Prob. of Profit (NPV > 0):** Out of all simulated futures (e.g., 5,000 paths), what percentage resulted in a positive NPV? A 75% probability means 3 out of 4 simulated futures were profitable.
* **Required Fleet Size:** The exact number of ASICs needed to fully saturate your Max Power Cap, accounting for PUE overhead.
* **Rev/kWh (Current):** The *Instantaneous Revenue Efficiency*. This tells you exactly how much USD is generated for every kilowatt-hour of electricity burned *today*. Compare this directly against your Energy Price ($/kWh).

### **The Charts**

* **Historical BTC Price vs. Revenue/kWh (Top Left):**
* *What it shows:* An overlay of the past year's Bitcoin price mapped against the implied Revenue per kWh of your selected hardware.
* *Context:* It includes a red dashed line representing your Fixed Energy Cost. If the green dashed line (Rev/kWh) dips below the red line, the facility is economically unviable. This chart proves how resilient your chosen hardware is against historical crypto crashes.


* **Cumulative Free Cash Flow (Bottom Left):**
* *What it shows:* A line chart of 100 sample "futures" over time. All lines start deep in the negative (representing your initial CAPEX purchase).
* *Context:* The white line is the median path. You are looking for the point where the white line crosses the $0 axis—this is your **Payback Period**. The spread of the faint blue lines shows the extreme upside and downside risks.


* **NPV Probability Distribution (Bottom Right):**
* *What it shows:* A bell-curve-style histogram of every single simulated project outcome.
* *Context:* A vertical red line sits at $0. You want the vast majority of the green bars to sit to the right of the red line. A "fat tail" on the left side warns you of severe downside risk if the market turns against you.
